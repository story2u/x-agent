# Audience: Overseas Chinese Youth

Use this reference when the user mentions 海外中文年轻人, 留学生, 海外职场人, working abroad, immigration, or cross-border life.

## Core Insight

Their money anxiety is often not only "income is not enough". It is a mixed feeling:

- income is converted through exchange rates in the head
- rent, deposit, utilities, and shared housing costs create baseline pressure
- cross-border transfers and fees make every move feel inefficient
- credit card cycles hide the real cost of the month
- subscriptions renew quietly
- food delivery, coffee, and rides feel small but repeat often
- friends split bills across currencies, apps, or delayed transfers
- FOMO spending feels social, not purely financial
- "reward myself" purchases come after stress
- they want to become better, but money seems to leak out before life improves

中文表达时优先使用这些具体词：信用卡账单、合租分账、朋友局 AA、汇率换算、跨境转账手续费、订阅自动扣费、外卖、咖啡、打车、奖励自己式下单。

## Required Scenes

For money-related long tweets, include at least two of these scenes:

- checking a credit card bill after avoiding it for days
- converting rent or salary back to RMB even when trying not to
- splitting utilities or dinner in a group chat
- noticing a subscription auto-renewal
- paying an international transfer fee
- buying coffee, delivery, or rides because the day felt too heavy
- hesitating before a FOMO social plan
- ordering something as a small reward after a hard week

## Tone Guidance

Speak like a warm operator who understands the life math. Do not shame spending. The content should restore agency:

- "不是让你抠，而是帮你把选择权拿回来。"
- "小钱不是罪，它只是需要被看见。"
- "今天的好运感，可能来自少漏一点。"
